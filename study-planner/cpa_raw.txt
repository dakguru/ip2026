**Q.1. According to the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, which of the following categories of persons is EXCLUDED from the definition of a "Consumer"?**

A. A person who buys goods for consideration paid in part and promised in part.
B. A person who avails of any service for deferred payment.
C. A person who obtains goods for resale or for any commercial purpose.
D. A person who uses goods with the approval of the buyer, exclusively for the purpose of earning his livelihood by means of self-employment.

**Correct Answer: C**
**Explanation:**
The Act defines a "consumer" but explicitly states it "does not include a person who obtains such goods for resale or for any commercial purpose". However, the explanation clarifies that "commercial purpose" does *not* include use by a person of goods bought and used by him exclusively for the purpose of earning his livelihood by means of self-employment.

**Q.3. Which of the following practices constitutes an "Unfair Contract" under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019?**

A. A contract requiring a security deposit that is manifestly excessive for the performance of contractual obligations.
B. A contract allowing the consumer to terminate the contract unilaterally with reasonable cause.
C. A contract where the trader refuses to accept early repayment of debts even without penalty.
D. A contract that imposes a penalty on the trader for breach of contract proportionate to the loss.

**Correct Answer: A**
**Explanation:**
An "unfair contract" includes terms "requiring manifestly excessive security deposits to be given by a consumer for the performance of contractual obligations". It also includes refusing to accept early repayment of debts *on payment of applicable penalty* and entitling a party to terminate unilaterally *without* reasonable cause.
